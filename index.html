About

Jaden Chin - Data Science and Economics

In the current digital age, college students’ music consumption is composed of various conscious and unconscious behavioral data that is a reflection of their preferences, engagement, and habits. This project aims to explore how a student’s music/listening statistics (i.e. average number of hours of music listened to in a day, top artists streamed) relates to the likelihood of purchasing concert tickets and how much they are willing to pay for a concert ticket. By analyzing these patterns, the project aims to uncover connections between listening statistics to consumer spending which can be valuable for companies within the entertainment and music industry in providing insights related to pricing/revenue strategy, marketing campaigns, and demand forecasting. Therefore, the overarching objective of this project is to bridge the gap between behavioral insights/consumer preferences and business decisions/applications within the entertainment/music space.
