Treemap

Overview

The treemap visualization shows the overall distribution of music genres across students and highlights the relationship between students' top genre choices and their school or academic background. By comparing the relative sizes of each genre category, the treemap helps reveal which genres are most dominant in the dataset while also showing how music taste may vary across student groups. This makes it useful for identifying both widely shared preferences and more distinct patterns tied to academic communities.

Analysis

The treemap reveals that while music preferences vary across schools, several genres, particularly Pop, R&B, and Hip-hop, consistently dominate across most student groups. Business and computer science students show especially strong concentrations in R&B and Pop, suggesting more mainstream listening patterns, while other colleges such as Arts and Science display a more balanced distribution across genres like Indie, Rock, and Rap. Despite these differences, the overall pattern indicates that student music taste is both broadly shared and slightly shaped by academic environment, with some schools exhibiting more diversity in genre preferences than others.
